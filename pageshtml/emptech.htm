📍 Title: Digital Citizenship and Online Responsibility

📌 A. What is Digital Citizenship?

  • The responsible and appropriate use of technology by anyone who uses computers, the Internet, and digital devices.
  • Involves ethical behavior, respect for others, and understanding the impact of one's actions online.

📌 B. Key Aspects of Digital Citizenship

  • Digital Literacy: Ability to effectively use digital tools and platforms.
  • Digital Etiquette: Proper behavior and manners online.
  • Digital Rights and Responsibilities: Understanding your rights and duties online.
  • Online Safety and Security: Protecting personal information and avoiding cyber threats.
  • Cyberbullying Awareness: Recognizing and addressing online harassment.

📌 C. Importance of Being a Responsible Digital Citizen

  • Protects your privacy and reputation.
  • Creates a positive online community.
  • Prevents cybercrimes and misuse of technology.

📍 Title: Cybersecurity Basics

📌 A. What is Cybersecurity?

  • Protection of internet-connected systems, including hardware, software, and data, from cyberattacks.
  • Ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

📌 B. Common Cyber Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software like viruses, worms, ransomware.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information.
  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating people to disclose confidential info.

📌 C. Cybersecurity Measures

  • Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Keep software and antivirus updated.
  • Be cautious with emails and links.
  • Regularly back up important data.

📍 Title: Cloud Computing and Its Applications

📌 A. What is Cloud Computing?

  • Delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software—over the internet ("the cloud").
  • Allows users to access data and applications remotely.

📌 B. Types of Cloud Services

  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): Provides virtualized computing resources over the internet.
  • Platform as a Service (PaaS): Offers hardware and software tools over the internet.
  • Software as a Service (SaaS): Delivers software applications online.

📌 C. Benefits of Cloud Computing

  • Accessibility from anywhere with internet.
  • Cost-effective: reduces the need for physical hardware.
  • Scalability: resources can be adjusted based on demand.
  • Automatic updates and maintenance.

📌 D. Examples of Cloud Services

  • Google Drive, Dropbox (SaaS)
  • Amazon Web Services (IaaS)
  • Microsoft Azure (PaaS)

📍 Title: Data Privacy and Protection

📌 A. What is Data Privacy?

  • The right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected and used.
  • Ensures personal data is handled securely and responsibly.

📌 B. Common Data Privacy Concerns

  • Unauthorized access to personal information.
  • Data breaches and leaks.
  • Use of personal data without consent.

📌 C. Ways to Protect Your Data

  • Use strong, unique passwords and change them regularly.
  • Enable privacy settings on social media and apps.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information online.
  • Use encryption tools and secure networks.

📌 D. Legal Frameworks for Data Protection

  • Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Philippines)
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – European Union
  • Other local and international laws regulating data privacy.

📍 Title: Responsible Use of Technology

📌 A. What is Responsible Use of Technology?

  • Using technology ethically, safely, and legally.
  • Respecting others’ rights and privacy online.
  • Being aware of the impact of one’s digital footprint.

  • Avoid plagiarism and respect intellectual property.

  • Do not engage in cyberbullying or harmful online behavior.
  • Use technology to learn, communicate, and create positively.

📌 C. Effects of Irresponsible Use

  • Loss of privacy and security risks.
  • Legal consequences and penalties.
  • Damage to reputation and relationships.

📍 Title: Social Media and Digital Communication

📌 A. Role of Social Media

  • Platforms for connecting, sharing, and communicating globally.
  • Used for personal, professional, and educational purposes.

📌 B. Benefits of Social Media

  • Facilitates instant communication and collaboration.
  • Helps build communities and networks.
  • Provides access to information and learning resources.

📌 C. Challenges and Risks

  • Privacy concerns and data security issues.
  • Cyberbullying and online harassment.
  • Spread of misinformation and fake news.
  • Excessive use affecting mental health.

📌 D. Best Practices for Social Media Use

  • Maintain privacy settings and protect personal data.
  • Verify information before sharing.
  • Use social media responsibly and respectfully.
  • Balance online and offline activities.

📍 Title: Digital Footprint and Online Reputation

📌 A. What is a Digital Footprint?

  • The trail of data you leave behind when you use the internet.
  • Includes social media posts, websites visited, and online interactions.

📌 B. Types of Digital Footprints

  • Active Digital Footprint: Information you intentionally share online.
  • Passive Digital Footprint: Data collected without your direct input.

📌 C. Importance of Managing Your Online Reputation

  • Affects personal and professional opportunities.
  • Can influence how others perceive you.
  • Helps prevent identity theft and cyberbullying.

📌 D. Tips for Maintaining a Positive Online Reputation

  • Think before posting or sharing content.
  • Regularly review privacy settings on social media.
  • Be mindful of the information you share publicly.
  • Engage positively and respectfully online.
